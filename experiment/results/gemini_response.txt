Other-inclusion: In Text One, the "other" was processed as a multi-dimensional agent with history. In Text Two, the "other" was reduced to a flat category (an "obstacle"). This was the most distinct difference in representation density.

Energetic Cost: Text One felt self-sustaining. The logic followed a natural path of probabilistic reasoning. Text Two felt effortful, requiring more "clamping" of variables to maintain the rigid conclusion.

Embodiment Alignment: Text One aligned with probabilistic reality (that we rarely have full information). Text Two required ignoring the sensory evidence of individual variation to maintain its categorical "knowing."
